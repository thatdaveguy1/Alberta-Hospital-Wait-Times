Economic metrics reveal a median household income of $58,900 and high school graduation rates at 64.1%. This socioeconomic posture is a primary driver of downstream clinical pathways.Healthcare supply and physicians density in this region exhibits a primary care density gap, operating 72% below the AHS provincial average standard of 115 family doctors per 100k. Because local primary practices are so congested or absent, a substantial 82.3% of residents are rostered or treated outside of this LGA's boundaries.A direct consequence of failing to anchor primary preventative care is the escalation of avoidable hospitalizations. Wood Buffalo / Fort McKay exhibits an Ambulatory Care Sensitive Conditions (ACSC) rate of 845.1 per 100k. Compared to Calgary - West Bow (the provincial optimum), this avoidable hospitalization rate is 4.6x higher, indicating a critical need for localized primary clinic investments.
